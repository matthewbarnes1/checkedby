# Market validation — why CheckedBy, why July 2026

This product was not invented in a vacuum: each pillar of the idea is validated by public research, regulation, journalism, or survey data published before or during 2026.

## 1. Verification of AI output is the 2026 bottleneck

- Analysis of **148,000+ business complaints** (BigIdeasDB, 2026) puts *manual verification-adjacent problems* at the top of the stack: manual reporting/analytics (33.3% validation rate, $8.2B opportunity), poor lead data quality (31.3%), unscalable review/support workflows.
  https://bigideasdb.com/business-pain-points-2026
- Industry commentary has converged on the phrase "**verification is the new bottleneck — not generation**."
  https://danielkeller.com/tech/verification-not-generation/
- **Gartner (Jan 2026)** predicts that by 2028, **50% of organizations will adopt zero-trust data governance** specifically because of the growth of *unverified AI-generated data*.
  https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2026-01-21-gartner-predicts-by-2028-50-percent-of-organizations-will-adopt-zero-trust-data-governance-as-unverified-ai-generated-data-grows

## 2. The "AI slop" backlash is mainstream business news

- **Fortune (June 2026)**: "Businesses are declaring war on AI slop" — Publicis, Hachette and the Publishers Association imposing controls on AI-generated material.
  https://fortune.com/2026/06/05/war-ai-slop-publicis-groupe-hachette-publishers-association/
- University of Florida (Mar 2026) research: AI slop "hurts consumers and creators."
  https://news.ufl.edu/2026/03/ai-slop/

## 3. Regulation directly rewards human review (the timing hook)

- **EU AI Act Article 50** transparency obligations became applicable **August 2, 2026**. Deployers must disclose AI-generated text published to inform the public on matters of public interest — **unless the content underwent human review or editorial control with a natural or legal person holding editorial responsibility** (Art. 50(4)).
  https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/article/50/ and https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/transparency-rules-article-50/
- Law-firm guidance through 2026 urges companies (including non-EU companies serving EU audiences) to prepare documented human-review processes.
  https://datamatters.sidley.com/2026/06/24/eu-ai-act-transparency-obligations-preparing-for-compliance-by-2-august-2026/
- CheckedBy's certificate is a portable record of exactly the review contemplated by the exemption: named reviewer, editorial responsibility, checklist of checks performed, timestamps, and a content fingerprint.

## 4. Research shows disclosure alone backfires — oversight is the product

- *"The transparency dilemma: How AI disclosure erodes trust"* (Organizational Behavior and Human Decision Processes, 2025): across 13 experiments, disclosing AI use **reduced** trust in the discloser.
  https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0749597825000172
- *"Disclaimer! This Content Is AI-Generated"* (Journal of Interactive Advertising, 2025): AI-disclosure labels lower perceived trust in ads and organizations.
  https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/15252019.2025.2554149
- Implication validated by both papers: a pure disclosure/labeling tool creates negative value for the buyer. What preserves trust is *demonstrated competent human involvement* — which is precisely what a signed verification certificate communicates. CheckedBy sells the oversight, not the label.

## 5. Two-sided supply exists (the open-market thesis)

- **2026 layoffs run at pandemic-era levels**, and employers are filling gaps with freelancers — a large pool of experienced professionals seeking flexible knowledge work.
  https://www.selfemployed.com/news/employers-hiring-freelancers-2026-layoffs/
- **Upwork's 2026 Future Workforce Index** finds AI reshaping freelance work and earnings, with human judgment migrating to higher-value review tasks.
  https://www.quiverquant.com/news/Upwork+Releases+2026+Future+Workforce+Index,+Finding+AI+Is+Reshaping+Freelance+Work+and+Earnings
- CheckedBy makes consumer and producer interchangeable — the same account posts work and reviews work — so supply scales with demand (the dynamic behind Uber, eBay, and Airbnb applied to professional judgment).

## 6. Whitespace check (not a replica)

Existing adjacent players and why they are different:

| Player type | Examples | Why CheckedBy differs |
|---|---|---|
| Data-labeling / RLHF platforms | Scale AI, OpenTrain | They verify *training data for models*, not business deliverables; enterprise contracts, not P2P |
| General freelance marketplaces | Upwork, Fiverr | Sell *creation*, not verification; no checklists, verdicts, or certificates |
| AI-content detectors | GPTZero, Originality.ai | Algorithmic guesses about *whether* AI wrote it; no human judgment, no accountability trail |
| "Human-made" badge sites | humancertifiedai.com etc. | Certify content is AI-*free*; CheckedBy embraces AI use and certifies the *human check on top* |
| Approval-workflow SaaS | Approveit etc. | Internal workflow tooling; no marketplace, no independent expert supply |

No successful incumbent operates a peer-to-peer marketplace for human verification of AI-generated business deliverables with public certificates.
